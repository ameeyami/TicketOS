# TicketOS — Positioning One-Pager

> **Category:** AI-native IT Service Management (ITSM)
> **One-liner:** The AI service desk you can **audit, undo, and afford**.

---

## Positioning statement

For **lean IT teams** drowning in routine requests, **TicketOS** is an AI-native
service desk where agents resolve tickets end-to-end — but unlike **ServiceNow**
(heavy, opaque, expensive) and newer **AI agents** (unproven, black-box), every
action is **reversible**, every resolution is **priced**, and every workflow
**earns its autonomy**.

---

## The problem

- **Legacy ITSM (ServiceNow):** months-long, consultant-dependent implementations;
  opaque pricing; AI bolted on as pricey add-ons billed in unpredictable "Assist"
  credits; overkill (cost + complexity) below ~500 agents; "UI/UX friction" a top
  complaint; AI quality gated on a clean CMDB you must build first.
- **New AI agents (e.g., Modern):** promise autonomous resolution, but are early and
  unproven — no public pricing, no visible trust/cost controls, no on-prem/regulated
  story, closed.
- **The shared gap:** everyone asks you to *trust the automation*. Nobody makes that
  trust **visible, reversible, and measurable**.

---

## The wedge: governed autonomy you can see and control

Five capabilities that — together — neither the incumbent nor the challenger offers:

1. **One-click rollback** — undo any action an agent took on a connected system
   (re-add the group, restore the account). *No competitor offers an undo button.*
2. **Cost-per-resolution ledger** — live $ per ticket and month-to-date burn against
   a **hard monthly budget**. *Directly answers ServiceNow's "Assist credit" billing
   anxiety and Modern's pricing opacity.*
3. **Dry-run blast-radius preview** — before a workflow runs, see exactly which
   systems it touches, what's reversible, and what pauses for approval.
4. **Earned autonomy** — workflows graduate `suggest → approve-each → auto-with-audit
   → full-auto` based on tracked success and rollback rates, and **tighten instantly**
   when trust drops.
5. **Chat-native intake** — requests arrive from Slack/Teams, get classified, and the
   agent replies in the same thread.

All on a foundation of **policy checks, approval gates, and a replayable audit trail**.

## Target customer

- **Who:** Lean IT / IT-operations teams, ~50–500 employees.
- **Buyer:** Head of IT / IT Ops lead.
- **Trigger:** Frustrated by ServiceNow's cost & complexity, or wary of trusting a
  black-box AI agent with production access.

---

## How it works (the loop)

```
Request (chat or portal)
  → AI triage & classify
  → policy check
  → approval gate (if earned autonomy / risk requires)
  → execute on real tools (Okta, Google Workspace, GitHub, Slack…)
  → audit + cost recorded · every action reversible
```

## Objection handling

| Objection | Answer |
|---|---|
| "Can I trust AI to act on production?" | Every action is **reversible**, **audited**, and gated by **earned autonomy** + approvals. |
| "Will AI costs spiral?" | **Hard monthly budget** + live **$/resolution** — no credit packs, no overage surprises. |
| "How long to implement?" | Opinionated defaults; usable by one IT generalist — not a dedicated admin team. |
| "Lock-in / black box?" | Transparent pricing, replayable audit, and you see (and can undo) everything. |
